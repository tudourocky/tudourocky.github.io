# 1B

The 1B experience will mainly focus on courses and the Waterloo campus during the summer.

## The UWP Experience

I was in residence for my first year and I was put in UWP WIS. It is a suite-style 3B1B residence. The suite comes with a kitchen, a fridge, and a couch, while the room comes with a bed, a closet, and a desk.

The room was fine in the fall given that both my roommates were clean and organized. The kitchen is a bit small for three people, but in my case, I worked around my roommates' schedules and relied heavily on my Instant Pot for cooking. So, I guess it worked out.

The biggest downfall was when the summer came. First of all, UWP does not have air conditioning, meaning that you have to survive the hot and humid summer that Waterloo offers. Not only that, the university somehow thought it was a great idea to have garbage pickup at 6 AM, where the forklift drivers can never put the forks into the holes on the garbage cans and they keep bumping into them. Not only that, they also thought it made sense to start lawn mowing at 7. Sometimes I would be sleeping with my windows open due to how hot the room becomes, and the next thing I know, the whole room is filled with dirt and the smell of grass.

So, after a while, I just stopped staying in my room and went to lecture rooms to study. However, as a dedicated gamer, I still spent a decent amount of time in my room with my friend playing League of Legends.

## The Campus

The campus is actually really nice in the summer. Due to the fact that there are way fewer people, you can actually go to DC at any random time and be able to find a seat. Not only that, having a lot more daylight allows you to go to places and spend time outside instead of just at home.

## 1B Courses

![Picture of lab](/images/1b.jpg)

### ECE 106

**Name of the course:** Electricity and Magnetism
**Rating:** 3/5

I think they have been making this course easier year by year. But even though it is made easier, it was still decently challenging for me. The problem is that I would think that I understood a concept, but then when given a question, I am not able to do it.

### ECE 108

**Name of the course:** Discrete Mathematics and Logic 1
**Rating:** 2/5

IDK, I did not understand this course at all so I really can't say much about it...

### ECE 124

**Name of the course:** Digital Circuits and Systems
**Rating:** 4/5

This course was taught to both the CEs and the EEs in the same lecture. So, we were in one of the big lecture rooms in RCH. The course content was pretty straightforward and as long as you do the past exams, it is really easy to get a good mark.

### ECE 140

**Name of the course:** Linear Circuits
**Rating:** 3/5

Dabbagh the GOAT gave us an inspirational speech on "if you can't do circuits, consider becoming a carpenter" :p. Course material-wise, it was pretty challenging for me as I was not taught circuits properly in high school. Ended up finding a video series on Bilibili that covered the whole course. The lectures were good, but once I fell behind there was no point in going as I would be completely lost anyways.

### ECE 192

**Name of the course:** Engineering Economics and Impact on Society
**Rating:** 2/5

Bird course, not much to say; you learn about engineering ethics and forget about them the next day. There are quizzes that were done on our own laptops in class.

### MATH 119

**Name of the course:** Calculus 2 for Engineering  
**Rating:** 4/5

Pretty easy course if you did AP Calc BC. Essentially, you are just learning how to do integrals.

## COOP

So essentially, I got both really lucky and unlucky for this co-op. The lucky part is that I was able to get matched with a job really early on despite only being ranked. But the bad part is that the company was not very organized and, despite being a startup, there wasn't much work that needed to be done. Plus, since it was fully remote, I wasn't able to make any meaningful connections either.

So from this experience, I learned that it is important to communicate clearly about the hiring manager's plan for their intern and get a sense of what you will actually do on the job. But at the same time, they could say that they have all these things that they would like the interns to do, but once you start they give you a completely different task.

## Random Tips

If you are in Waterloo in the summer, make sure you have an umbrella on you at all times as it tends to start pouring in the middle of the day along with thunder and lightning.
